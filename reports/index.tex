\section{Introduction}

The initial requirement of the project is to build a platform that works like a streaming service, but for education. From that requirement, the system must allow learners to browse available content, watch lessons online, and access materials in a smooth and organized way. The platform should also support clear content categorization, easy navigation, and a responsive interface so that users can learn from different devices. In addition, the project should provide a strong technical foundation for managing content, handling user access, and supporting future growth.

Beyond the technical requirement, the project is also based on the idea that streaming platforms have become an important part of modern learning. Today, many students and independent learners prefer video-based lessons because they are flexible, accessible, and easier to follow than traditional text-only materials. Learning streaming platforms make it possible to study at any time, repeat lessons when needed, and access content from different locations. This makes them especially valuable in online education, self-paced learning, and skill development.

Because of this trend, building a learning streaming platform is both relevant and practical. It combines the strengths of digital media delivery with the needs of modern education. A platform such as modlearn can help connect instructors and learners through organized video content, while also improving the overall learning experience. For this reason, the project is not only a software development task, but also an exploration of how streaming technology can be adapted to educational purposes.

This report will explain the background of the project, the requirements that guided its development, and the role of streaming platforms in the learning environment. It will also discuss how the system is designed and implemented to meet those goals.
