\subsection{Non-functional requirements}

The non-functional requirements of ModLearn define the quality attributes that the system must satisfy in addition to its functional behavior.

\begin{longtable}{L{1.7cm}L{10.1cm}L{2.0cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{ID} & \textbf{Requirement Statement} & \textbf{Verify} \\
\midrule
\endfirsthead
\toprule
\textbf{ID} & \textbf{Requirement Statement} & \textbf{Verify} \\
\midrule
\endhead
\bottomrule
\endfoot
NFR-01 & The system shall protect protected learner and administrator operations through authenticated session handling and role-based authorization. & Inspection and test \\
NFR-02 & The system shall validate request inputs and response structures consistently at the application boundary to reduce invalid or unsafe data exchange. & Inspection and test \\
NFR-03 & The system shall restrict cross-origin access according to configured server policy and shall allow only approved origins, headers, and methods. & Inspection and test \\
NFR-04 & The system shall provide controlled file access through signed upload and download operations rather than unrestricted direct storage access. & Inspection and test \\
NFR-05 & The system shall maintain data integrity through relational constraints, unique keys, foreign keys, and domain validation rules within the database schema. & Inspection and test \\
NFR-06 & The system shall support continuity and acceptable response behavior for repeated learning activity such as course browsing, lesson resume, and continue-watching flows. & Test \\
NFR-07 & The system shall remain usable across common desktop and mobile screen sizes through a responsive web interface. & Test \\
NFR-08 & The system shall be maintainable through modular separation of concerns across authentication, routing, service logic, validation, and persistence layers. & Inspection \\
NFR-09 & The system shall support extensibility so that additional modules, routes, and data features can be added without redesigning the entire platform structure. & Inspection \\
NFR-10 & The system shall provide implementation-level testability through automated tests for core modules, routers, services, and storage behavior. & Inspection and test \\
NFR-11 & The system shall provide API discoverability and integration support through generated OpenAPI documentation for exposed service routes. & Inspection and test \\
NFR-12 & The system shall preserve operational availability by initializing required backend dependencies, including storage resources, before normal media operations are performed. & Inspection and test \\
\end{longtable}

\subsection{Database Structure}

The database structure of ModLearn is designed as a relational database management system (RDBMS) to support the main operations of the platform in a consistent and organized manner. This database stores information required by the software, beginning with user and authentication data and extending to course content, lesson structure, categories, media files, pricing records, purchases, learning progress, and course reviews.
